Brown Bears

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of
northern Eurasia and North America. Its conservation
status is currently Least Concern

There are many subspecies within the brown bear
species, including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan
brown bear.

Brown bears are omnivorous and feed on berries, plant
roots and shoots, small mammals, fish, calves of many
hoofed animals, and carrion. They often cache food in
shallow holes, and they dig readily and vigorously in
search of rodents.
